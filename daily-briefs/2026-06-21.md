## Executive Summary

Iran's IRGC re-declared the Strait of Hormuz closed on 20 June, invoking continued Israeli strikes in Lebanon as a violation of MOU Article 1, days after the June 17 signing. CENTCOM disputes physical enforcement, reporting vessels still transiting, but the declaratory closure re-activates war-risk insurance triggers and commercial operator hold postures. Burgenstock implementation talks opened 21 June with Lebanon added as an emergency first-session agenda item; Iran nearly walked out following Trump social-media threats before agreeing to continue. UKMTO confirmed a Houthi ballistic-missile attack on MV Transworld Navigator in the Gulf of Aden on 21 June, demonstrating maintained Houthi maritime capability independently of the Iran-US track. Diplomatic trajectory remains cautiously positive — both delegations engaged — but the IRGC re-closure and Lebanon fragility represent material implementation risk.
