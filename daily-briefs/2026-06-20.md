## Executive Summary

Iran and the US signed the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding on 17–18 June 2026, with Pakistan announcing that the MOU implies Tehran will promptly reopen the Strait of Hormuz and the US blockade will cease immediately. CENTCOM lifted port restrictions 18 Jun and the Joint Maritime Information Center issued a Hormuz routing advisory. However, physical reopening stalled: a Saudi VLCC trickle reversed on 19 Jun (0 outbound vessels), the Lloyd's/Chubb war-risk consortium launched 19 Jun (positive but explicitly not a JWC listed-area change), and the planned Bürgenstock follow-on signing ceremony was cancelled after Israeli strikes in Lebanon threatened to derail talks. An Israel-Hezbollah ceasefire was agreed 19–20 Jun mediated by US, Qatar and Iran, reducing but not eliminating escalation risk.
