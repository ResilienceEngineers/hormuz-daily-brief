## Executive Summary

CENTCOM completed its third strike wave of the week (140 targets — missile/drone sites, naval capabilities, ammunition storage, communication networks, coastal surveillance) in response to the IRGC's attack on the Cyprus-flagged GFS Galaxy in the Strait of Hormuz, leaving one crew member missing. Iran formally re-declared the Strait of Hormuz closed "until further notice." Following the third wave, Iran launched its largest multi-state retaliation to date: ballistic missiles and drones targeting Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Jordan, UAE, and Oman's Musandam exclave. CENTCOM confirmed a fourth wave on Sunday 12 Jul evening (5 p.m. ET). Brent rose to ~$76–78. Araghchi–Busaidi Oman talks 11 Jul produced an Oman two-corridor draft proposal; Iran conditioned US–Iran direct-talk resumption on prior Hormuz compliance. Mojtaba Khamenei did not make a confirmed public appearance at the 10–12 Jul Mashhad memorial.
