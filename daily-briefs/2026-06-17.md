## Executive Summary

The US–Iran MOU was electronically signed by Vance and Ghalibaf on Sunday 15 June, with Trump physically co-signing at Versailles on the evening of 17 June alongside Iranian President Pezeshkian's digital signature — Pakistan's PM confirms the MOU enters force "with immediate effect." The 14-point text, released publicly by NBC News and CBS on 17 June, mandates Hormuz commercial traffic restart "immediately" with demining within 30 days. However, zero AIS commercial transits were recorded as of 17 June morning; Maersk surcharges remain in force; and the operational gap between diplomatic signature and physical gate-opening is confirmed. Three friction points: (1) 30-day demining deadline vs. Pentagon 6-month assessment; (2) Israel explicitly non-party, IDF remaining in Lebanon; (3) 60-day nuclear clock extendable but carrying Trump's "go back to bombing" reservation. Brent ~$79.52 (17 Jun) pricing partial reopening with lag.
