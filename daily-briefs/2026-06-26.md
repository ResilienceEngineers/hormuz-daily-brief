## Executive Summary

The IMO **paused its evacuation of 11,000 stranded seafarers** on 25 June following the Ever Lovely attack in the Gulf of Oman — the first cargo vessel struck since the MOU ceasefire took effect. A US official attributed the attack to the IRGC, which had hours earlier declared only its northern route authorised through the Strait of Hormuz, rejecting the Oman/IMO southern corridor as uncoordinated and unsafe. Vessel transits reached a single-day high of 70 on 25 June, but the dual-corridor authority dispute means commercial operators face competing mandatory routing instructions from two sovereign authorities with no agreed rules. Round 1 Burgenstock talks (21–22 Jun) produced a roadmap and de-confliction cell but no concrete corridor framework. Brent crude fell ~$74/bbl, down ~19% over 30 days, as Saudi/Gulf export restarts market-priced the peace dividend ahead of operational confirmation.
