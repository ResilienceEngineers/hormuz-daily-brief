## One-liner

IRGC rejects IMO/Oman southern Hormuz corridor as "unacceptable and dangerous"; at least two tankers U-turn; Rubio begins GCC tour to sell MOU to sceptical Gulf allies as IAEA inspection dispute sharpens.

## Executive Summary

Day 118: Operational friction in the Strait intensified as the IRGC formally rejected the IMO/Oman-announced southern transit corridor, insisting all vessels use only Tehran-designated routes or face enforcement action. At least two tankers reversed course near Khasab, Oman (Lloyd's List Tier 1). Simultaneously, Secretary of State Rubio began a three-day Gulf tour (UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain) to reassure sceptical GCC allies that the Islamabad MOU does not unduly favour Iran. An acute IAEA dispute emerged: IAEA DG Grossi asserted inspections "will happen" under the MOU, while Iran's deputy FM Gharibabadi explicitly stated inspections will only resume after a final deal and sanctions relief — contradicting both Grossi and Trump's claim of inspections "into infinity." Lebanon Round 5 talks opened in Washington 23–25 Jun; fragile IDF-Hezbollah ceasefire held nominally but Israeli strikes continued in southern Lebanon. Five additional South Korean vessels transited Hormuz, signalling marginal easing at the state-operator level.

## 01 · Maritime Security — Severe (→)

**Signals:**
- IRGC rejected Oman/IMO southern corridor on 25 Jun: "unacceptable and dangerous." Azumasan and Blue Star I performed U-turns near Khasab.
- 70 MarineTraffic crossings on Wed 25 Jun (wartime high); 31 on Tue 24 Jun. Most used southern Oman coast route.
- IMO evacuation plan (600 ships, 11,000 seafarers) launched 23 Jun; 40 ships in first wave; 500–600 total in operation.

**Why it matters:** Two-tier strait operating in practice: IRGC-controlled northern route vs. Oman/US southern route. IRGC enforcement posture means no commercially insurable transit outside Tehran-approved routes. IMO evacuation plan now operationally contested.

**Supply-chain implication:** Hold non-IMO-sequenced transits. War-risk remains 8×; 6 P&I clubs withdrawn. IMO evacuation is the only safe exit mechanism for stranded vessels.
