## Executive Summary

Day 131 marked the sharpest single-day escalation since the June 17 MOU signing. IRGC forces struck three commercial vessels transiting the Omani-route corridor — a Qatari LNG carrier (Al Rekayyat), a Saudi crude tanker (Wedyan), and a third vessel — triggering CENTCOM's largest strike wave since late June: 80+ precision targets including 60+ IRGC small boats, coastal radar, and anti-ship missile sites across Bandar Abbas, Qeshm, and Sirik. Iran retaliated with drone and missile strikes on US bases in Bahrain and Kuwait. US Treasury revoked General License X, ending authorised Iranian oil sales. Trump at the NATO Ankara summit declared the ceasefire "over." NATO adopted a declaration calling on Iran to respect Hormuz freedom of navigation. Brent surged 5% to ~$78/b. The MOU is in acute breach from both sides.
