# Sources — Hormuz Daily Brief

Sources are tiered by **track record and signal density**, not prestige. A source's tier can move up or down based on calibration findings logged in `backtest-log.md`.

The Taleb principle applies: a few sources with strong track records over many sources with broad coverage. Each tier defines a **role** in the brief, not a hierarchy of importance.

---

## Tier 1 — Primary, official, verifiable

These are first-party operational sources. A claim sourced here counts as **Hard** signal.

### Maritime / operational
- **UKMTO (UK Maritime Trade Operations)** — `ukmto.org` — incident reports, JMIC advisory notes, recent-incidents page. **The single most important source for the brief.**
- **MARAD MSCI advisories** — `maritime.dot.gov/msci` — US Maritime Administration security advisories.
- **US NAVCENT / 5th Fleet** — `cusnc.navy.mil` — official press releases, deployment confirmations.
- **IMO (International Maritime Organization)** — `imo.org` — formal statements on freedom of navigation issues.
- **EUNAVFOR Aspides** — Red Sea/Bab el-Mandeb mission reports.

### Government / state
- **US State Department** — `state.gov` — daily press briefings, readouts.
- **White House** — `whitehouse.gov` — Presidential statements (weight as Soft unless followed by ExecOrder).
- **OFAC / Treasury** — `home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/financial-sanctions` — sanctions designations (Hard).
- **UK FCDO** — `gov.uk/government/organisations/foreign-commonwealth-development-office`
- **EU EEAS** — `eeas.europa.eu` — High Representative statements.
- **Iran SNSC / IRGC official** — Iranian state communiqués via state news agencies. Treat as **primary for Iranian position**, not as fact about external reality.

## Tier 2 — Specialized data and analysis with operational track record

Subscription/commercial sources with direct measurement capability. Where paywalled, the brief uses public summaries and credits the data origin. Counts as **Hard** signal when data is quantitative and verifiable.

- **Kpler** — `kpler.com` — vessel-level energy flow data. Industry standard for tanker tracking.
- **Vortexa** — `vortexa.com` — energy cargo flow analytics.
- **Lloyd's List Intelligence** — `lloydslistintelligence.com` — tanker and shipping intel.
- **TankerTrackers.com** — vessel-level tracking, often first to spot changes in dark fleet behavior.
- **ICC International Maritime Bureau (IMB)** — `icc-ccs.org/icc/imb` — Piracy Reporting Centre.
- **JMIC (Joint Maritime Information Centre)** — published via UKMTO — combined situational picture for the region.
- **S&P Global Platts / Commodity Insights** — `spglobal.com/commodityinsights` — energy pricing and flow.
- **Argus Media** — `argusmedia.com` — energy markets.
- **Marine Traffic / VesselFinder** — public AIS for spot-checks.

## Tier 6 — Excluded by default (NOISE unless triangulated)

These sources can occasionally surface a real signal but the noise rate is too high to use them as a basis for any brief claim. They are checked only as **leads** that must be confirmed via Tier 1–3 before inclusion.

- Anonymous OSINT accounts on X/Telegram (pseudonymous, no track record audit)
- Op-eds and speculation pieces (regardless of outlet)
- "Sources say" or "sources told us" without attribution
- Reposts of state media without independent confirmation
- AI-generated summaries (including but not limited to syndicated AI news bots)
- Influencer commentary on social media
- Forum/Reddit/Discord posts

A claim originating in Tier 6 only enters the brief if it has been independently confirmed by ≥1 Tier 1–3 source.

## Conflict-of-interest disclosure

Several Tier 4 sources have commercial positions in commodity markets. The brief reports their public commentary as analytical input, not as advocacy. Where a source has known exposure relevant to a claim (e.g., a tanker company commenting on insurance costs), this is flagged inline.

State outlets (Tier 5 regional, Tehran Times etc.) are reported as **positions of the state**, not as factual reporting. The brief never says "Iran said X" without naming the outlet and treating it as an Iranian regime communication.
