        Methodology, sources, and about

        How this brief is produced and what kinds of inputs it uses.

        Methodology

        This brief uses a tiered signal-weighting framework. Only verified physical events from primary sources can move the threat assessment. Trend movement requires confirmed change versus the prior 72 hours. The system continuously calibrates against outcomes — when predictions miss, the underlying heuristics are sharpened. The full operating ruleset is kept private to preserve analytical edge.

        Sources · six tiers

        • Tier 1 · Primary & official. Maritime authorities, defense commands, foreign ministries, treasury sanctions desks.
        • Tier 2 · Specialized commercial intelligence. Vessel-tracking platforms (AIS-based), aircraft-tracking platforms (ADS-B), tanker-flow analytics, energy-cargo intelligence, piracy-reporting bureaus.
        • Tier 3 · Analytical institutions. Geopolitical and security think tanks with rigorous publication standards.
        • Tier 4 · Commercial analysts & columnists. Independent practitioners and commodity-desk analysts with multi-year track records.
        • Tier 5 · Wire services & reputable regional press. Western wires, top business and political dailies, regional papers (with editorial-line caveats).
        • Tier 6 · Excluded by default. Anonymous OSINT, op-eds, social media, AI-generated summaries — admitted only after Tier 1–3 corroboration.
