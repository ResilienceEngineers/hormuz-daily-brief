Two days of indirect US–Iran technical talks in Doha concluded 2 July with Qatar and Pakistan confirming "positive progress" on MOU implementation issues; next round deferred until after Khamenei funeral ceremonies (4–9 Jul). The core structural disputes are unchanged: Iran insists on corridor sovereignty and service fees after the 60-day MOU window, while the US treats Hormuz as an international waterway requiring Gulf-state endorsement. 01

Maritime & Chokepoints

Severe → Confidence: High (Kpler/MARAD/IMO Tier 1-2)
  • Kpler recorded 34 verified crossings on 30 Jun in Hormuz — "broad mix of commercial, energy-linked, and support traffic" but routing "fragmented across Iranian and Omani routes" (RFERL/Kpler, Tier 1/2). Traffic increasing but well below ~100/day pre-crisis baseline.
  • Foreign-flagged vessel (ARISTA, OFAC-sanctioned, Shamkhani network) reported aground north of Hormuz Island on 1 Jul after departing Iran-approved lane — Iranian state media framed as warning to non-compliant operators (CBS/Fox/AP, Tier 1).
  • MARAD 2026-006 and JMIC "Substantial" threat level in force. IMO listed 49 confirmed incidents in Hormuz/Middle East as of 30 Jun. Mine hazard: incomplete demining per Kpler/IMO; no commercial insurer underwrites uncertified transits.

Why it matters: The dual-corridor dispute (Iran: south-of-Larak; Oman/IMO: Musandam-hugging) is the operational blocker — not the ceasefire per se. Iran's sovereign-control claim preserves asymmetric leverage regardless of MOU language.

Supply-chain implication: Cape-only routing mandatory for uncertified operators. War-risk premium persists. Two-tier market (risk-tolerant operators vs mainstream carriers) limits overall throughput recovery.

Military & Kinetic

Elevated → Confidence: Medium (Axios/CNN/CENTCOM Tier 1)
  • US–Iran kinetic pause holds as of 2 Jul. Axios reports US official language: understanding to "keep things quiet for the coming week" — implying ~6 Jul as natural expiry point coinciding with Khamenei funeral.
  • MH-60S Sea Hawk helicopter (USS George H.W. Bush) emergency water landing in Arabian Sea 2 Jul; one crew member missing; "no indication the emergency was caused by hostile action" per NAVCENT (CNN live, Tier 1).
  • Israeli Def. Min. Katz stated Mojtaba Khamenei "marked for death"; Iran FM Araghchi warned "immediate powerful response" to any threat against leadership. IDF struck Manzala area (southern Lebanon) 1 Jul — contained, no full-scale resumption (CNN/Axios Tier 1).

Why it matters: The kinetic pause is a tactical instrument, not a structural resolution. Pause expiry ~6 Jul falls during Khamenei funeral (4–9 Jul), creating a compounded risk window: IRGC may have reduced offensive tempo but retains capability; any Israeli action against Mojtaba could trigger immediate breakout from pause.

Supply-chain implication: Pause is necessary but not sufficient for commercial resumption. An incident during funeral window would immediately invalidate current routing risk calculus and bounce Brent $5–10.

Diplomatic

Elevated ↑ Confidence: Medium-High (Qatar FM/Axios/Reuters Tier 1)
  • Qatar FM Ansari and Pakistan FM confirmed "positive progress" on MOU implementation issues after 2-day Doha talks (1–2 Jul); parties "agreed to continue discussions...with the next meeting to be scheduled at the earliest possible time following the funeral processions" (CBS/Qatar FM, Tier 1).
  • Iran Dep. FM Gharibabadi: no direct US–Iran meetings; emergency communication channel to be established by 3 Jul. $3bn frozen assets first tranche discussed — "purchase of humanitarian goods" mechanism reportedly agreed in principle (Axios, Tier 1); US officials denied formal agreement.
  • Assembly of Experts public split on MOU: 60+ of 84 members issued joint statement on MOU clauses; body's own secretariat rebuked the procedural irregularity within hours — signals growing internal Iranian institutional infighting over MOU red lines (Iran Int'l, Tier 2).

Why it matters: "Positive progress" on MOU implementation disputes — not nuclear talks. Parties are still arguing over commitments they already signed, not the final deal (Axios: "two weeks into that window they're still arguing over MOU terms"). MOU Day 16/60. Internal Iranian Assembly of Experts fracture is a new structural signal.

Supply-chain implication: Next Doha session timing (post-funeral = earliest ~10 Jul) extends MOU implementation gap. Any frozen asset release signals Iran extracting value from diplomatic track — prolongs MOU window but does not resolve Hormuz governance.

Infrastructure & Energy

Elevated ↓ Confidence: High (Trading Economics/Investing.com Tier 2-3; EIA Tier 1)
  • Brent ~$70.89/bbl (2 Jul intraday, Investing.com); WTI ~$67.89 — below $68 reported for Thursday (Trading Economics). Worst quarterly drop since 2020. Market pricing supply glut: Iran shipped >40mb since blockade lifted; Russia at record export levels (Trading Economics, Tier 2/3).
  • EIA June STEO (9 Jun) assumed Hormuz largely closed and projected $105/bbl in June–July — actual now ~32% below that forecast, confirming partial re-opening and supply-buffer effects. Next EIA STEO: 7 Jul.
  • Mine hazard uncertified; no commercial insurer underwrites strait transits. Oman Malacca-model service-fee proposal under US review — if adopted post-MOU window, structurally reprices all Gulf cargo.

Why it matters: The price collapse decouples the security premium from the operational reality — physical disruption remains severe while paper pricing has normalised. This creates divergence risk: if Brent falls below $70 and then re-escalation occurs, the bounce is sharper and supply-chain hedges positioned at current levels are inadequate.

Supply-chain implication: Q3 hedge window shifted down to $65–75 range. Review floor cover before 7 Jul EIA update. Any Hormuz re-escalation represents $5–10 upside; supply-glut narrative limits sustained spike unless full re-closure.

Alternative Routes

Severe → Confidence: High (MARAD/EUNAVFOR/UN SC Report Tier 1)
  • Red Sea: ~26 ships/week vs ~80 baseline. Houthi pause holds since MOU (17 Jun); no confirmed UKMTO commercial vessel strike in reporting period. Houthis retain full strike capability; MARAD 2026-006 active to 22 Sep 2026; UN SC Secretary-General report (9 Jun): no new incidents 9 May–9 Jun (UN SC Report, Tier 1).
  • EUNAVFOR ASPIDES extended to 28 Feb 2027 (confirmed, Skuld/Tier 3 citing EUNAVFOR). CENTCOM NAVCENT coordination ongoing. IRGC Quds Force Gen. Qaani warned of coordinated Axis of Resistance "security belt" from Hormuz to BaM if escalation resumes (UN SC Report Jun 2026, Tier 1).
  • Cape of Good Hope near-saturation: Sohar/Fujairah/Salalah congestion; 10–14d transit premium; DHL previously flagged 4–6 month normalisation timeline. Oman service-fee model (Malacca blueprint) would structurally add cost to any Hormuz-direct resumption.

Why it matters: Dual-chokepoint structure means any Cape-only routing decision locks in a 10–14d premium until both corridors certify. The Houthi conditional pause is the fragile second element — a single UKMTO-confirmed strike reactivates the full dual-chokepoint problem.

Supply-chain implication: Cape-only mandatory through EOD 9 Jul minimum. Plan for sustained Q3 premium. Monitor Houthi posture closely during funeral window — Iran/IRGC re-escalation would likely lift Houthi operational restraint.

Methodology

This brief uses a tiered signal-weighting framework. Only verified physical events from primary sources can move the threat assessment; trend movement requires confirmed change versus the prior 72h. The system continuously calibrates against outcomes — when predictions miss, the underlying heuristics are sharpened. The full operating ruleset is kept private to preserve analytical edge against adversarial reading.

Sources

Inputs span six tiers, weighted by track record. Specific outlets and platforms are not disclosed publicly.

  • Tier 1 — Primary & official. Maritime authorities, defense commands, foreign ministries, treasury sanctions desks. First-party communiqués that count as Hard signal.
  • Tier 2 — Specialized commercial intelligence. Vessel-tracking platforms (AIS-based), aircraft-tracking platforms (ADS-B), tanker-flow analytics, energy-cargo intelligence, piracy-reporting bureaus. Operational data with measurable track records.
  • Tier 3 — Analytical institutions. Geopolitical and security think tanks with rigorous publication standards and demonstrable calibration history on this region.
  • Tier 4 — Commercial analysts & columnists. Independent practitioners and commodity-desk analysts whose published positions can be back-tested over multi-year horizons.
  • Tier 5 — Wire services & reputable regional press. Western wire services, top-tier business and political dailies, regional papers across the Gulf, Israel, and Iran (read with editorial-line caveats), and analytical defense-and-policy publications.
  • Tier 6 — Excluded by default. Anonymous OSINT accounts, op-eds, social media, "sources say" without attribution, AI-generated summaries, forum content. Enter the brief only after independent corroboration by Tier 1–3.
