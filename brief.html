Trump entered the White House Situation Room on 29 May to make a "final determination" on the Iran MOU — a 60-day ceasefire extension covering Hormuz reopening and nuclear talks — but concluded the ~2-hour meeting without announcing a decision (CNN live blog). Neither Trump nor Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei has formally signed off, while Iran's Foreign Ministry confirmed the MOU is "not yet finalized" and Iran's IRGC-linked Fars quoted "final stages of approval" on the Iranian side. UKMTO issued a Critical advisory on 29 May and the US Treasury sanctioned Iran's Persian Gulf Strait Authority (PGSA), which vowed to continue tolling operations "without interruption." Brent crude closed at ~$91.37 on 29 May — down 19% in May, market pricing deal proximity — but analysts warn any reopening will be slow given mines, damaged infrastructure, and insurance reset requirements.

01

Maritime & Hormuz

Severe → Confidence: High
  • UKMTO Critical advisory issued 29 May: "maritime security threat level remains CRITICAL due to ongoing military conflict"; mariners warned of "increased naval presence" and "enhanced force protection postures" (UKMTO — Tier 1).
  • US Treasury sanctioned Iran's Persian Gulf Strait Authority (PGSA) 28 May; PGSA vowed to continue operations "without interruption" — confirms IRGC toll-enforcement mechanism remains operational despite sanctions (CNN — Tier 1).
  • IRNA (Iranian state media) reported 23 IRGC-authorised vessel transits in past 24 hours — these are state-controlled transits under PGSA regime, not free commercial shipping; PortWatch recorded only ~4 transits/day as of 24 May (Tier 5 / IMO PortWatch — Tier 2 crosscheck).

Why it matters: The PGSA sanctions + continuity-vow dynamic creates a sequencing problem for MOU implementation: even if both sides sign, IRGC enforcement infrastructure remains in place and sanctioned, meaning the "no tolls, no harassment" clause requires simultaneous PGSA stand-down and US sanction-waiver — a coordination challenge not resolved in the MOU text.

Supply-chain implication: Do not pre-position for Hormuz reopening ahead of physical mine-clearance confirmation (MOU 30-day window post-signing) and insurance reinstatement. Even optimistic scenario: commercial carriers return no earlier than late July 2026. Plan Cape of Good Hope routing through August.

Sources: UKMTO advisory 29 May 2026 (Tier 1); CNN live blog 29 May 2026 (Tier 1); straits.live / IMO PortWatch (Tier 2); IRNA via straits.live (Tier 5 — treated as IRGC position).

Supply-chain implication: Kuwait Mina al-Ahmadi interceptor depletion (~75%) remains the highest near-term energy infrastructure risk. Gulf-state refinery exposure should be stress-tested for a multi-day outage scenario through June 5.

Methodology

This brief uses a tiered signal-weighting framework. Only verified physical events from primary sources can move the threat assessment; trend movement requires confirmed change versus the prior 72h. The system continuously calibrates against outcomes — when predictions miss, the underlying heuristics are sharpened. The full operating ruleset is kept private to preserve analytical edge against adversarial reading.

Sources

Inputs span six tiers, weighted by track record. Specific outlets and platforms are not disclosed publicly.

  • Tier 1 — Primary & official. Maritime authorities, defense commands, foreign ministries, treasury sanctions desks. First-party communiqués that count as Hard signal.
  • Tier 2 — Specialized commercial intelligence. Vessel-tracking platforms (AIS-based), aircraft-tracking platforms (ADS-B), tanker-flow analytics, energy-cargo intelligence, piracy-reporting bureaus. Operational data with measurable track records.
  • Tier 3 — Analytical institutions. Geopolitical and security think tanks with rigorous publication standards and demonstrable calibration history on this region.
  • Tier 4 — Commercial analysts & columnists. Independent practitioners and commodity-desk analysts whose published positions can be back-tested over multi-year horizons.
  • Tier 5 — Wire services & reputable regional press. Western wire services, top-tier business and political dailies, regional papers across the Gulf, Israel, and Iran (read with editorial-line caveats), and analytical defense-and-policy publications.
  • Tier 6 — Excluded by default. Anonymous OSINT accounts, op-eds, social media, "sources say" without attribution, AI-generated summaries, forum content. Enter the brief only after independent corroboration by Tier 1–3.
