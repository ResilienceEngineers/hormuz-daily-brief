Six categories

01

Maritime & Chokepoints

Crisis ↑ Confidence: High
  • UKMTO reported a vessel on fire 8nm northwest of Kumzar, northern Oman, on 19 Jul; IRGC claimed two "non-compliant" tankers were targeted after attempting to cross the southern Hormuz route.
  • US Energy Secretary Wright stated approximately two-thirds of pre-conflict traffic — about 14 mb/d combined — is moving via Hormuz and the bypass route; strait-only flow is estimated at just under 7 mb/d per day.
  • Three sources told Reuters that Iran has instructed the Houthis to close the Red Sea to shipping if the US attacks Iran's power network; a source close to the Houthis confirmed drones and missiles are deployed near Bab al-Mandeb awaiting an activation order.

Why it matters: The Hormuz IRGC enforcement action is escalating from warning shots to active vessel targeting along the bypass route through Omani waters — the only remaining path for non-Iranian-cleared shipping. Any confirmed US strike on Iranian power infrastructure activates the Houthi Bab al-Mandeb order within hours, collapsing the last functioning energy export corridor from the Gulf.

Implication: Zero tolerance for Hormuz or Red Sea direct routing. The vessel-on-fire UKMTO incident of 19 Jul is the first confirmed IRGC action on the Omani-waters bypass route and should be treated as a hard signal that the "safe corridor" assumption no longer holds. Dual-chokepoint lock-in is now a near-term operational reality, not a tail risk.

Sources: UKMTO (Tier 1) 19 Jul; CENTCOM.mil (Tier 1) 19 Jul; Reuters via FDD (Tier 1/2) 16 Jul; CNN (Tier 1) 19 Jul; US Energy Dept statement (Tier 1) 19 Jul.
02

Military Operations

Crisis ↑ Confidence: High
  • CENTCOM launched Wave 9 at 19:00 ET on 19 Jul and completed it at 22:00 ET, marking the ninth consecutive night of strikes; CENTCOM stated strikes "will continue degrading Iranian military capabilities used to attack commercial vessels."
  • Iran's Atomic Energy Organization said the US struck the Darkhovin nuclear power plant construction site; the IAEA confirmed it is investigating and that the facility was in its "very early stages" of construction with no nuclear material on site.
  • A US official told ABC News that US fighter jets stationed in the United Kingdom and Germany are being sent to the Middle East , signalling force build-up for sustained campaign.

Why it matters: Wave 9 extends the geographic breadth of the campaign to Tabriz in the northwest — 1,300km from Bandar Abbas — indicating a deliberate widening of the target envelope beyond coastal maritime assets. The Darkhovin strike, even at a construction site, crosses a politically salient threshold: it is the first US strike on a nuclear-related Iranian facility since the war's resumption and will harden Iranian domestic opinion against negotiation. Reinforcement from European-based fighter jets indicates the White House is preparing for an extended campaign beyond the initial nine-night cadence.

Implication: P(Wave 10 tonight) = 0.85. P(US strike on operational Iranian power infrastructure by EOD 22 Jul) upgraded to 0.55 (↑5pp from Day 142 baseline). The four-condition heuristic for escalated target sets (KIA + political mandate + 9-night pattern + reinforcement deployment) is fully met.

Sources: CENTCOM.mil (Tier 1) 19 Jul; CNN live blog (Tier 1) 19 Jul; ABC News (Tier 1) 19 Jul; IAEA statement (Tier 1) 19 Jul; Iran International (Tier 2) 19 Jul.
03

Diplomatic / Political

Severe → Confidence: Medium
  • Secretary Rubio said Sunday the US "always remains open to a diplomatic solution," adding the US has "tried multiple times with Iran" and would continue; he was departing for Manila for the ASEAN summit.
  • Iran FM Araghchi said security shortcomings from the war's opening strikes still exist, and that Tehran must negotiate if "absolute military victory" is impossible — but talks should occur only when Iran has "the upper hand." He also noted he has yet to meet new Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei.
  • Iraq's PM Al-Zaidi is expected to visit Tehran next week, with Iraq's Foreign Ministry saying the visit will include signing memoranda of understanding in areas of common cooperation — the only visible back-channel thread in the 20 Jul window.

Why it matters: The Rubio "open to diplomacy" signal is Soft-tier (words, no confirmed session). The Araghchi "negotiate from upper hand" framing reflects a regime posture that requires Iran to show military strength before talks — the opposite of the current trajectory. The Iraq back-channel is the sole potential conduit but has no confirmed US content. Counter-signal to current trend: Araghchi's public acknowledgement that absolute military victory is impossible is the first senior Iranian official statement in the resumption cycle that opens a rhetorical door to negotiation — but it is conditional and unverified as Hard signal.

Implication: P(New diplomatic framework by EOD 25 Jul) = 0.05 (↓1pp). Iraq back-channel may slow the escalation clock marginally; insufficient to alter trend assessment. MOU remains void with no counter-offer. P(replacement framework within 30 days) ≤ 0.08 per standing heuristic.

Sources: CNN live blog (Tier 1) 19 Jul; Middle East Eye live blog (Tier 1/2) 20 Jul; ABC News (Tier 1) 19 Jul; Iran International (Tier 2) 19 Jul.
04

Infrastructure & Energy

Crisis ↑ Confidence: High
  • Brent crude reached $90.42 on 20 Jul, up 2.63% on the day — the highest level since the ceasefire collapse.
  • Kuwait's power and desalination plant was attacked for a second consecutive day on 19 Jul. The Kuwait Petroleum Corporation had confirmed that "one of the vital sites in the oil sector was subjected… to repeated, brutal Iranian attacks, resulting in severe material losses along with some injuries."
  • Iran's Gharibabadi condemned the Darkhovin nuclear power plant construction site strike as "a dangerous assault on Iran's peaceful infrastructure" and warned Iran would "take appropriate measures"; the Atomic Energy Organization confirmed the site was struck.

Why it matters: Oil prices have risen more than 14% in the week of 17 Jul as hostilities intensified. Brent breaking $90 on 20 Jul puts the $95 threshold — the level at which Houthi dual-chokepoint activation becomes fully priced in — within a single escalatory event. The Darkhovin strike elevates Iranian political motivation to authorise the Houthi power-grid trigger, even though Darkhovin itself poses no radiological risk and is not an active power plant.

Implication: P(Brent >$95 by EOD 22 Jul) upgraded to 0.60 (↑8pp from Day 142). P(Brent >$90 by EOD 21 Jul) = effectively confirmed at current trajectory. Refined product margins under pressure; long-tenor supply contracts on Red Sea routing should be treated as force-majeure-exposed.

Sources: Yahoo Finance / NYSE Mercantile (Tier 3) 20 Jul; Trading Economics (Tier 3) 17 Jul; Reuters via Fox News (Tier 1/2) 18 Jul; CNN (Tier 1) 19 Jul; ABC News (Tier 1) 19 Jul.
05

Alternative Routes

Severe ↑ Confidence: High
  • Yanbu loadings reached approximately 4.7 mb/d around 13 Jul, up from 3.36 mb/d on 10 Jul; loadings have averaged above 4 mb/d since June 2026, compared with approximately 973,000 bpd in the same period of 2025.
  • Saudi Arabia has substituted Hormuz exposure for Bab al-Mandeb exposure; there is no equivalent bypass route for Bab al-Mandeb — if this corridor is interdicted at scale, no pre-engineered alternative routing solution exists for Yanbu's export volumes.
  • The Giants Brigades intercepted a boat in the Bab al-Mandeb carrying weapons-manufacturing components for Houthi rebels on 16 Jul, following a tracking operation targeting a smuggling network linked to Iran-backed Houthis; attempts to smuggle drone and missile manufacturing components into Yemen are increasing.

Why it matters: Roughly 7% of global oil output transited Bab al-Mandeb in June; Saudi Arabia has been rerouting exports from the Persian Gulf to the Red Sea via its east-west pipeline, with shipments departing from Yanbu. The weapons-intercept at Bab al-Mandeb on 16 Jul confirms active Iranian logistics to the Houthis are ongoing despite EUNAVFOR ASPIDES presence — the pre-positioning is not degraded. Cape routing remains the only fully reliable fallback but adds ~$1M/voyage and ~10 days.

Implication: P(Houthi formal Bab al-Mandeb commercial ban or anti-shipping strike by EOD 22 Jul) = 0.45 (↑5pp from Day 142 P=0.55 at EOD 21 Jul gate; maintaining elevated but noting no activation through 20 Jul 06:00 UTC). Accelerate Cape buffer stress-test to 50-day cover. Any US strike on operational Iranian power grid moves this to P=0.90+ within 6 hours.

Sources: Reuters via FDD / Long War Journal (Tier 1/2) 16 Jul; The National (Tier 2) 16 Jul; Discovery Alert / Reuters (Tier 2/3) Jul 2026; Security Council Report (Tier 2) Jul 2026.

Outlook

Crisis ↑ Confidence: High
  • Nine consecutive nights of CENTCOM strikes; geographic envelope widening to Tabriz (NW Iran) and Darkhovin (nuclear-adjacent, SW Iran) — both new in Wave 9.
  • Red lines set by both sides have been crossed; a return to full-scale war that would further destabilise the Middle East and disrupt the world economy appears increasingly likely.
  • Araghchi signals conditional negotiating openness; Iraq back-channel only active diplomatic thread; P(framework by 25 Jul) = 0.05.

Why it matters: The structural picture has deteriorated materially since Day 141. MOU void, mediators struck, nuclear-adjacent infrastructure targeted, 17 US KIA, Brent $90+, Houthi trigger pre-positioned — every constraint layer that previously capped escalation has been removed or degraded. The sole remaining brake is the distinction between operational power-grid strikes and the construction-site strike at Darkhovin. Iran's Darkhovin response ("dangerous assault") signals that if the US crosses to operational power infrastructure, the Houthi activation order follows automatically.

Implication: Scenario C (re-escalation / dual-chokepoint lock-in) is the modal path at 92%. Scenario B (frozen attrition) at 6% requires the Iraq back-channel to produce a verified signal. Scenario A (negotiated framework) at 2% requires a public US–Iran joint statement — no observable foundation for that exists within the 30-day horizon.

Methodology

This brief uses a tiered signal-weighting framework. Only verified physical events from primary sources can move the threat assessment; trend movement requires confirmed change versus the prior 72h. The system continuously calibrates against outcomes — when predictions miss, the underlying heuristics are sharpened. The full operating ruleset is kept private to preserve analytical edge against adversarial reading.

Sources

Inputs span six tiers, weighted by track record. Specific outlets and platforms are not disclosed publicly.

  • Tier 1 — Primary & official. Maritime authorities, defense commands, foreign ministries, treasury sanctions desks. First-party communiqués that count as Hard signal.
  • Tier 2 — Specialized commercial intelligence. Vessel-tracking platforms (AIS-based), aircraft-tracking platforms (ADS-B), tanker-flow analytics, energy-cargo intelligence, piracy-reporting bureaus. Operational data with measurable track records.
  • Tier 3 — Analytical institutions. Geopolitical and security think tanks with rigorous publication standards and demonstrable calibration history on this region.
  • Tier 4 — Commercial analysts & columnists. Independent practitioners and commodity-desk analysts whose published positions can be back-tested over multi-year horizons.
  • Tier 5 — Wire services & reputable regional press. Western wire services, top-tier business and political dailies, regional papers across the Gulf, Israel, and Iran (read with editorial-line caveats), and analytical defense-and-policy publications.
  • Tier 6 — Excluded by default. Anonymous OSINT accounts, op-eds, social media, "sources say" without attribution, AI-generated summaries, forum content. Enter the brief only after independent corroboration by Tier 1–3.
