The two-day Doha technical round concluded 2 July with "positive progress" but no breakthrough: a communications channel to flag MOU violations was agreed and a $3 bn frozen-asset goods-purchase mechanism was sketched, while the toll/corridor sovereignty dispute and nuclear-inspection schedule remain unresolved. Iranian negotiators left Doha immediately; all diplomacy is now paused until after the six-day Khamenei funeral (4–9 July), with the next session to be scheduled "at the earliest possible time" afterwards. The kinetic stand-down is holding into Day 17, but the funeral window — compounded by CENTCOM's confirmation of a second Marine Expeditionary Unit (11th MEU, USS Boxer ARG) now operating in the Middle East and Iran's army warning against strikes during the procession — represents the highest discrete risk window of the current MOU period. Mojtaba Khamenei is unlikely to appear publicly, per his representative in India, raising latent leadership-legitimacy uncertainty that could affect IRGC command cohesion post-funeral.

Six categories

01

Maritime & Chokepoints

Severe → Confidence: High
  • Iran issued fresh Hormuz route-compliance warning 2 Jul: vessels not following Tehran-designated routes face "immediate and firm response from armed forces" (CNN live, Tier 1, 2 Jul). IRGC asserting joint sovereignty with Oman; toll/fee dispute unresolved.
  • ~27 ships/day transiting vs ~84/day baseline; convoys moving under naval escort; IMO evacuation remains paused — no safety guarantee granted, no corridor language agreed at Doha (CSIS/Straits.live, Tier 2/3, 2–3 Jul).
  • Mine hazard status uncertified; war-risk insurance premiums at 1–5% of hull value vs 0.25% pre-war; no commercial underwriter active for uncertified transits (Al Jazeera/BIMCO, Tier 1/3, 28 Apr 2026).

Why it matters: Iran's 2 Jul warning reaffirms that the strait is operationally contested even during the kinetic pause. The comm-channel agreed at Doha reduces miscalculation risk marginally, but corridor governance — who authorises transit, under what terms, with what safety guarantee — remains the central unresolved structural blocker to commercial resumption.

Supply-chain implication: Cape-only mandatory through EOD 9 Jul minimum. Two-tier Hormuz market (risk-tolerant sovereign-flagged vs mainstream commercial) persists. Any funeral-window incident bounces Brent $5–10 and suspends even risk-tolerant transits. IMO evacuation restart is the leading indicator to watch for Wave 3 normalization.

Military & Kinetic

Elevated → Confidence: High
  • Kinetic stand-down holds into Day 17 (since ~17 Jun MOU). No confirmed IRGC or US/Israel kinetic incident as of 06:00 UTC 3 Jul. CENTCOM confirmed 11th MEU (USS Boxer ARG, ~2,000+ Marines) now operating in Middle East — second MEU in theater (CENTCOM/CNN, Tier 1, 2 Jul).
  • US Navy MH-60S Sea Hawk helicopter (USS George H.W. Bush-assigned) made emergency landing in Arabian Sea 2 Jul; one crew member missing; search ongoing. Not attributed to hostile action but occurring in contested operating environment (CNN/ABC, Tier 1, 2 Jul).
  • Iran army general warned US and Israel against "any attack during the funeral procession" (CNN 3 Jul, Tier 1). Israeli FM Katz "marked for death" threat against Mojtaba still active. Iranian FM Araghchi: "immediate powerful response" to any Israeli strike.

Why it matters: The funeral window (4–9 Jul) is the highest discrete risk gate of the MOU period. Two compounding factors: (1) IRGC command cohesion uncertainty with Mojtaba not appearing publicly; (2) Israeli PM Netanyahu not bound by MOU, and Defense Minister Katz has issued explicit threats. The 11th MEU deployment signals US is maintaining military leverage in parallel with diplomacy.

Supply-chain implication: Kinetic pause necessary but not sufficient. A single incident during funeral window bounces Brent $5–10, triggers UKMTO advisory escalation, and suspends the post-funeral Doha session. Hold all non-pre-cleared transits through EOD 9 Jul.

Sources: CENTCOM (Tier 1, 2 Jul) · CNN live (Tier 1, 2–3 Jul) · ABC News (Tier 1, 2 Jul) · Iran army statement via semi-official Tasnim (Tier 5, treated as Iranian regime position)
03

Diplomatic

Elevated → Confidence: High
  • Doha round (1–2 Jul) concluded: Qatar/Pakistan joint statement confirmed "positive progress"; communications channel to flag MOU violations agreed; $3 bn frozen-asset goods-purchase mechanism agreed in principle (Central Bank of Iran to buy humanitarian goods — no cash transfer). Next session "at earliest possible time" after funeral (Al Jazeera/The National/CNN, Tier 1, 2 Jul).
  • Corridor/toll dispute unresolved: Vance stated "no tolls in Strait of Hormuz" (ABC/CNN, Tier 1, 2 Jul); Iran's Ghalibaf asserted Tehran will not negotiate further until MOU terms implemented; IRGC's Akbarzadeh reiterated "full control" of Hormuz. Nuclear agenda not reached at Doha — Vance: "going to start talking about that."
  • Mojtaba Khamenei unlikely to appear at funeral per his India representative Hakim Elahi (India Today/ANI/Tribune India, 3 Jul): "security doesn't allow him to come." Only confirmed bilateral meeting with any official: Pezeshkian direct meeting (~2.5h). No new images/audio of Mojtaba since Feb 28.

Why it matters: Doha produced incremental confidence-building outputs (comm channel, asset mechanism sketch) but left the three structural blockers — corridor governance, tolls, and nuclear inspections — entirely unresolved. MOU is at Day 17/60; with the funeral pause consuming ~7 of the remaining 43 days, the effective negotiating window is tightening. Mojtaba's continued absence creates ambiguity about who holds final authority on IRGC decisions, adding a non-linear risk factor.

Supply-chain implication: Next decisive gate: post-funeral session (~10 Jul). If session convenes with corridor + nuclear working groups on joint agenda, Scenario A probability rises. If deferred further, Implementation Drag (Scenario B) deepens and MOU suspension risk (Scenario C) grows.

Sources: Al Jazeera (Tier 1, 2 Jul) · The National (Tier 1, 2 Jul) · CNN live (Tier 1, 2–3 Jul) · ABC News (Tier 1, 2 Jul) · India Today/ANI (Tier 2, 3 Jul) · Tribune India/ANI (Tier 2, 3 Jul) · Qatar FM/X (Tier 1, 2 Jul)

Infrastructure & Energy

Elevated ↓ Better Confidence: Medium
  • Brent ~$71–72/bbl (3 Jul); WTI ~$68.4 — lowest levels since pre-war (late February). Market pricing diplomacy success and partial supply normalization. Brent has surrendered essentially all war premium since MOU signing. EIA STEO due 7 Jul — key recalibration event (Trading Economics/Forbes/HDFCSky, Tier 2/3, 2–3 Jul).
  • UAE exports restored to 3.9 mb/d; Saudi Arabia exports at ~90% of pre-war; total Hormuz flows claimed >10 mb/d with US military support. Kuwait production at 1.65 mb/d vs 580k/d in May. Supply surplus emerging from multi-source production rebound + Iran exports >40 mb since blockade lifted (Trading Economics, Tier 2/3, 3 Jul).
  • War-risk insurance: 1–5% hull value vs 0.25% pre-war. Mine hazard uncertified — Al Jazeera/BIMCO: "sweeping the Strait clear of mines with complete certainty could be a near-impossible task." Physical reopening and paper price have diverged significantly; Q3 hedge window $65–80.

Why it matters: The price collapse creates a divergence risk — physical disruption remains severe (mine hazard, route compliance demands, IMO evacuation paused) but paper Brent has normalized. This divergence suppresses the market signal that supply chains need to maintain contingency posture. EIA STEO 7 Jul may recalibrate demand outlook.

Supply-chain implication: Q3 hedge window: $65–80 range. Review contracts before 7 Jul EIA STEO. Any funeral-window kinetic incident bounces Brent sharply — the gap between current price and disruption-scenario pricing is now ~$15–25/bbl, creating significant tail risk for unhedged positions.

Sources: Trading Economics (Tier 2/3, 2–3 Jul) · Forbes (Tier 2, 2 Jul) · HDFCSky/Reuters (Tier 2, 3 Jul) · Al Jazeera/BIMCO (Tier 1/3, 28 Apr) · EIA (Tier 1)

Alternative Routes

Severe → Confidence: High
  • Red Sea: Houthi pause holds since MOU 17 Jun — no UKMTO-confirmed vessel strike as of 3 Jul. Skuld (Tier 3): "Houthis have not resumed attacks since the start of the current Iran-US-Israel confrontation." Group retains full capability (missiles, aerial drones, naval USVs). MARAD 2026-006 active.
  • EUNAVFOR ASPIDES mandate extended to February 2027. Cape of Good Hope: near-saturation; 10–14 day transit premium vs pre-crisis; diversion around Cape adds ~11,000 nm and 10 days. Most major carriers maintaining Cape-only posture.
  • Suez Canal at ~26 ships/week (vs ~250/day pre-Gaza-crisis baseline); Oman Larak corridor active but contested by IRGC patrols; Saudi Yanbu pipeline active but capacity-limited. Dual-chokepoint constraint structurally unchanged.

Why it matters: Houthi pause is an important de-escalation signal but is fragile — dependent on MOU holding and on Houthi leadership's continued calculation that restraint serves Iran's interests. The funeral window (4–9 Jul) is a potential trigger for Houthi opportunism if IRGC signals shift. No alternative route offers equivalent capacity to pre-crisis Hormuz + Red Sea combined.

Supply-chain implication: Cape-only mandatory through EOD 9 Jul minimum. Buffer inventory draw continues for Hormuz-dependent Gulf exporters. Alt-route saturation premium persists. Any Houthi resumption post-funeral triggers immediate Cape capacity crisis.

Sources: Skuld (Tier 3) · MARAD 2026-006 (Tier 1) · EUNAVFOR ASPIDES (Tier 1) · Wikipedia Red Sea crisis (Tier 3, Jul 2026) · UKMTO (Tier 1)

Outlook & Scenarios

Elevated / Pivoting → Confidence: Medium
  • Scenario A (Negotiated framework): 37% (↓1pp vs Day 125). Doha produced incremental steps but not corridor language. MOU window narrowing — Day 17/60 effective; funeral pause consumes ~7 more days. Nuclear talks not yet started.
  • Scenario B (Implementation drag): 46% (↑1pp, modal). Comm channel + asset mechanism = confidence-building outputs. Structural blockers — corridor, tolls, Lebanon, IAEA — remain intact. Post-funeral session ~10 Jul is next decisive gate.
  • Scenario C (Re-escalation / MOU collapse): 17% (→). Funeral window 4–9 Jul = elevated tail risk. Mojtaba non-appearance adds IRGC command-cohesion uncertainty. Katz "marked for death" threat active. Vance: "can't commit" to no return to combat.

Counter-signal to current trend assessment: Brent at pre-war levels and UAE/Saudi exports normalizing suggest markets see higher probability of durable peace than this brief's 37% Scenario A. Either the brief is over-weighting structural blockers, or markets are under-weighting implementation risk. This divergence is worth monitoring as an independent signal.

Supply-chain implication: Modal scenario (B) implies: Cape-only routing maintained; cost equilibrium holds; buffer inventory drawdown continues but not yet breach; next review gate 10 Jul post-funeral session. Scenario C tail: Threat 4→5; alt routes saturate; price rationing in refined products; immediate FM exposure on long-tenor contracts.

Methodology

This brief uses a tiered signal-weighting framework. Only verified physical events from primary sources can move the threat assessment; trend movement requires confirmed change versus the prior 72h. The system continuously calibrates against outcomes — when predictions miss, the underlying heuristics are sharpened. The full operating ruleset is kept private to preserve analytical edge against adversarial reading.

Sources

Inputs span six tiers, weighted by track record. Specific outlets and platforms are not disclosed publicly.

  • Tier 1 — Primary & official. Maritime authorities, defense commands, foreign ministries, treasury sanctions desks. First-party communiqués that count as Hard signal.
  • Tier 2 — Specialized commercial intelligence. Vessel-tracking platforms (AIS-based), aircraft-tracking platforms (ADS-B), tanker-flow analytics, energy-cargo intelligence, piracy-reporting bureaus. Operational data with measurable track records.
  • Tier 3 — Analytical institutions. Geopolitical and security think tanks with rigorous publication standards and demonstrable calibration history on this region.
  • Tier 4 — Commercial analysts & columnists. Independent practitioners and commodity-desk analysts whose published positions can be back-tested over multi-year horizons.
  • Tier 5 — Wire services & reputable regional press. Western wire services, top-tier business and political dailies, regional papers across the Gulf, Israel, and Iran (read with editorial-line caveats), and analytical defense-and-policy publications.
  • Tier 6 — Excluded by default. Anonymous OSINT accounts, op-eds, social media, "sources say" without attribution, AI-generated summaries, forum content. Enter the brief only after independent corroboration by Tier 1–3.
