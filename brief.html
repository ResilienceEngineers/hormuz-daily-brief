Day 133 opens with the US–Iran MOU structurally breached from both sides but with a fragile back-channel still alive. CENTCOM conducted two confirmed strike waves (80+ targets 7 Jul; ~90 targets 8 Jul) against IRGC boats, air defenses, coastal radar, and missile infrastructure across southern Iran and Tehran; a third wave on the night of 9 Jul was reported by Iranian state media but not confirmed by CENTCOM as of 06:01 UTC. The IRGC retaliated against Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar (first acknowledged strike), and Jordan's Al-Azraq base. Ali Khamenei was buried at Mashhad's Imam Reza Shrine in the early hours of 10 Jul; successor Mojtaba Khamenei remained entirely absent — the funeral period's last gate for a public appearance has now closed without one, deepening command-opacity concerns. A critical counter-signal: Trump stated Iran contacted US officials seeking a new deal after the strikes resumed, and Pakistan and Qatar confirmed continued mediation efforts; technical talks on the MOU remain the acknowledged US posture even mid-strike-cycle. Brent crude oscillates in the $77–79 range as markets reassess whether supply disruption will prove durable, with the Hormuz corridor reduced to ~13 vessels per 24 hours vs. ~110/day pre-war.

01

Maritime & Chokepoints

Crisis ↑ Confidence: High
  • Hormuz: 13 vessel transits in prior 24h (7 inbound, 6 outbound) per MarineTraffic cited by CNN 9 Jul — 88% below pre-war ~110/day baseline; GPS spoofing elevated in strait vicinity (CNN 9 Jul).
  • Maersk Sentosa explosion confirmed by Maersk corporate 9 Jul (northernmost Gulf of Aden / Nishtun); Houthis claimed ballistic + wing missiles; no crew injury; vessel continued voyage. Wikipedia corroborated.
  • UKMTO 5 Jul: bulk carrier attack 30nm SW Hodeidah (unclaimed); Minervagracht Dutch cargo ship ablaze Gulf of Aden after explosive device (EUNAVFOR ASPIDES confirmed); Houthi claim Wednesday.

Why it matters: For the first time since March 2026, Hormuz and Bab al-Mandeb face simultaneous active incidents, compressing the alternative-route buffer. The Iranian toll-corridor remains the only navigable Hormuz lane, giving IRGC de facto gate control even without a formal closure declaration.

Implication: No viable non-Iranian-controlled Hormuz corridor. Dual chokepoint stress means Cape rerouting absorbs both Gulf and Red Sea displaced cargo simultaneously — Salalah/Jeddah congestion will intensify. Any operator with Q3 Gulf-origin cargo must price in Cape-route lead times of +14 days and 8× war-risk premium through at minimum EOD 17 Jul.

Sources: CNN/MarineTraffic (Tier 1) 9 Jul; Maersk corporate advisory (Tier 2) 9 Jul; UKMTO JMIC Update 067 (Tier 1) 5 Jul; Wikipedia Houthi attacks log (Tier 3); EUNAVFOR ASPIDES (Tier 1).
02

Military Activity

Crisis ↑ Confidence: High
  • CENTCOM Day 1 (7 Jul): 80+ targets — 60+ IRGC small boats, coastal radar, anti-ship missiles (CENTCOM.mil official release). CENTCOM Day 2 (8 Jul): ~90 targets — air defense, coastal surveillance, missile/drone storage, naval logistics along Iran's coastline including Tehran (CENTCOM.mil; NPR; Al Jazeera).
  • IRGC retaliation 9 Jul: Bahrain (Juffair, Sheikh Isa), Kuwait (Arifjan, Ali Al-Salem; 14 projectiles intercepted; 1 injured), Qatar (early-warning system — first acknowledged by IRGC; Qatari authorities silent), Jordan Al-Azraq (10 ballistic missiles; 8 intercepted; first Jordan disclosure since June 11). IRGC: "if US continues, will expand to other Gulf bases."
  • Night 9 Jul: Iranian state media reported fresh explosions in Bushehr; CNN reported USS Abraham Lincoln crews at full readiness; US officials denied Iranian claims of new CENTCOM strikes as of ~01:00 EDT 10 Jul — unresolved as of 06:01 UTC.

Why it matters: The IRGC has now confirmed strikes on five separate Gulf-state host nations in two days — a geographic escalation ladder that was absent in the pre-MOU period. The expansion to Jordan and Qatar's acknowledged targeting raises the threshold for Gulf-state tolerance and introduces NATO-adjacency dynamics (Jordan hosts French, German, Belgian forces at Azraq).

Implication: Supply-chain operators with Gulf-state manufacturing or distribution hubs (UAE currently quiet but targeted in prior phases) must reassess business-continuity posture. The USS Abraham Lincoln's operational readiness signals a third CENTCOM strike wave remains on standby; the "strike-pause-negotiate" pattern noted by a US official (CNN) is the current operational heuristic but not a commitment.

Sources: CENTCOM.mil (Tier 1) 7–8 Jul official releases; NPR (Tier 1) 9 Jul; Al Jazeera (Tier 1) 9 Jul; RFERL live blog (Tier 2) 9 Jul; CNN live blog (Tier 1) 9–10 Jul; OAN/CENTCOM statement (Tier 1 via Tier 4) 9 Jul.

Diplomatic Track

Severe → Confidence: Medium
  • Iran contacted US officials seeking new deal after strikes resumed — Trump public statement (NBC/AP); "I don't know if they're worthy of making a deal" (NBC); US official confirmed: "still committed to finding a resolution; technical talks continue" (CNN 9 Jul).
  • Pakistan–Qatar active mediation: FM Araghchi called Pakistan Army Chief Munir (Iran FM called strikes "clear violation" of MOU); also called Turkish and Omani FM; Pakistan FO: "no alternative to dialogue" (Express Tribune 9 Jul; RFERL 9 Jul). CNN: "Pakistan and Qatar working to bring parties back to negotiating table" (regional sources).
  • Ali Khamenei buried Mashhad 10 Jul early morning (IRNA official; Fox News 10 Jul); Mojtaba Khamenei absent entire funeral period — disfigured in Feb 28 strike; security services limiting exposure; written statements only (Reuters/AP via Spokesman-Review; BusinessToday 10 Jul). Condolence ceremony Sat 11 Jul: Tasnim reports Mojtaba will lead prayers — potential first public appearance.

Why it matters: Iran's proactive contact with Washington post-strikes is the single most significant counter-signal against Scenario C this cycle. It confirms pragmatists within the Iranian system retain some agency over IRGC hardliners, and that the MOU framework has not been formally renounced despite the kinetic exchange. The Ghalibaf public defiance (Tier 2/Soft) is rhetoric; the FM-to-Army Chief call to Pakistan (Tier 2, Hard-adjacent) is structural signalling.

Implication: Scenario B (frozen attrition) survivable if the back-channel produces a confirmed session within 7 days post-burial. The 60-day MOU deadline (17 Aug) remains the nominal clock. Mojtaba's Saturday appearance is the highest-value single observable this week: visible = some command coherence; absent = IRGC-dominant decision loop, reducing negotiating reliability.

Sources: CNN (Tier 1) 9–10 Jul; NPR (Tier 1) 9 Jul; Express Tribune Pakistan (Tier 2) 9 Jul; RFERL (Tier 2) 9 Jul; Fox News (Tier 2) 10 Jul; BusinessToday (Tier 3) 10 Jul; Tasnim via Tribune India (Tier 3) 10 Jul; Al Jazeera (Tier 1) 9 Jul.

Infrastructure & Energy

Severe ↑ Confidence: High
  • Brent crude: settled ~$77–78 on 9 Jul (from $78 spike on 8 Jul); intraday range 9 Jul $76.77–$79.54 (Investing.com); WTI ~$73.5; market partially reassessing durable disruption given back-channel signals, but structurally elevated from pre-crisis ~$72 level (Trading Economics 9 Jul; Yahoo Finance 10 Jul at $78.01).
  • GL-X (General License X) revoked 7 Jul — no new Iranian oil purchases authorised; pre-7 Jul cargoes in transit allowed grace period with proceeds to blocked interest-bearing accounts (Al Jazeera/CNBC 8 Jul). Trump threatened Kharg Island — Iran's primary crude export terminal handling ~90% of exports — not yet struck.
  • CENTCOM strikes 8 Jul included Chabahar (maritime control tower + depot), Tehran, Konarak, Iranshahr airport (partial IRGC Aerospace); Iran confirmed Aq Qala railway bridge struck. Bushehr nuclear plant: strikes in vicinity (Iranian local official via IRNA); CENTCOM did not confirm; plant undamaged per prior pattern (NPR 9 Jul).

Why it matters: Brent has not breached $80 despite two confirmed CENTCOM strike waves and GL-X revocation — markets are pricing a non-zero back-channel probability. A Kharg Island strike would be the single largest supply-shock event since the war began, adding potentially 1.5–2.0 mb/d of export risk to an already constrained market.

Implication: Maintain Q3 Brent hedge in $76–82 band. GL-X revocation means any pre-7 Jul Iranian crude cargoes in transit have uncertain clearance; legal exposure for buyers must be assessed against grace-period terms. Kharg strike watch (W5) is the key upside energy catalyst through EOD 17 Jul.

Sources: Trading Economics (Tier 2) 9 Jul; Investing.com (Tier 2) 10 Jul; Yahoo Finance (Tier 2) 10 Jul; Al Jazeera (Tier 1) 8 Jul; NPR (Tier 1) 9 Jul; CNBC (Tier 1) 8 Jul; CENTCOM.mil (Tier 1) 8 Jul.

Alternative Routes

Severe → Confidence: High
  • Maersk Sentosa (US-flagged) struck by Houthi ballistic + wing missiles near Nishtun/Gulf of Aden 9 Jul — confirmed by Maersk corporate; no crew injury; vessel safe; Houthis claimed (Wikipedia; Maersk.com). First Houthi confirmed attack on a Maersk vessel since campaign resumed.
  • Minervagracht (Dutch cargo) struck Gulf of Aden — ablaze, crew helicopter-evacuated (EUNAVFOR ASPIDES/MarineLink). Houthis claimed cruise missile. Second simultaneous Red Sea/GoA incident in 24–48 hours.
  • UKMTO 5 Jul JMIC Update 067: bulk carrier attack 30nm SW Hodeidah unclaimed; Suez Canal transits continuing normally; GNSS interference near Yanbu elevated (no operational impact); Saudi East–West pipeline operational; EUNAVFOR ASPIDES mandate extended Feb 2027 (UKMTO Tier 1).

Why it matters: The resumption of confirmed Houthi kinetic activity against commercial vessels — specifically targeting US-flagged Maersk — while the Hormuz corridor is simultaneously at 88% collapse creates the first genuine dual-chokepoint supply-chain scenario of this war phase. The relief valve that Gulf-origin cargo relied on (Cape → Suez) is now itself under attack pressure.

Implication: Cape-only routing now carries both elevated transit time (+14 days) and elevated attack risk through the Gulf of Aden approach. Operators should assess whether full Cape bypass (avoiding GoA/Bab al-Mandeb entry) is operationally viable. Salalah/Jeddah congestion will worsen as rerouted volume concentrates. 30-day inventory buffer review for Gulf-origin feedstocks is now structurally required, not precautionary.

Sources: Maersk corporate advisory (Tier 2) 9 Jul; Wikipedia Houthi attacks log (Tier 3); MarineLink/EUNAVFOR (Tier 1) 9 Jul; UKMTO JMIC Update 067 (Tier 1) 5 Jul; CNBC (Tier 1) 5 Jul.

Outlook & Scenarios

Crisis ↑ Confidence: Medium
  • Scenario C (Re-escalation/MOU collapse) remains modal at 52% (↓3pp from 55% yesterday) — marginal softening driven solely by Iran's back-channel contact and Pakistan–Qatar continued mediation; no structural de-escalation event observed.
  • Scenario B (Frozen attrition, back-channel survives) at 38% (↑3pp) — conditioned on confirmed technical session within 7 days, no formal blockade order, and no Kharg strike; all three conditions unverified as of 06:01 UTC.
  • Counter-signal noted (methodology requirement): Trump stated Iran "agreed to just about everything we need" in a Thursday statement (Jerusalem Post/ToI 10 Jul) — but context unclear and talks on final deal not yet begun; Trump also said he "wasted a lot of time" negotiating with Iran in same cycle. Signal is mixed; insufficient to move Scenario A above 10%.

Why it matters: The 72h window (7–10 Jul) has introduced both the sharpest kinetic escalation since February 28 and a back-channel signal. This dual dynamic — strike-and-talk — is consistent with the Scenario B "coercive bargaining" pattern that preserved the June MOU through multiple prior crisis cycles.

Implication: The 17 Aug MOU deadline is the structural clock. If a confirmed technical session is not announced within 7 days of burial (by 17 Jul), probability will shift further toward Scenario C. The Mojtaba Saturday appearance (W1) and formal blockade order watch (W4) are the two highest-value near-term observables for re-weighting.

Methodology

This brief uses a tiered signal-weighting framework. Only verified physical events from primary sources can move the threat assessment; trend movement requires confirmed change versus the prior 72h. The system continuously calibrates against outcomes — when predictions miss, the underlying heuristics are sharpened. The full operating ruleset is kept private to preserve analytical edge against adversarial reading.

Sources

Inputs span six tiers, weighted by track record. Specific outlets and platforms are not disclosed publicly.

  • Tier 1 — Primary & official. Maritime authorities, defense commands, foreign ministries, treasury sanctions desks. First-party communiqués that count as Hard signal.
  • Tier 2 — Specialized commercial intelligence. Vessel-tracking platforms (AIS-based), aircraft-tracking platforms (ADS-B), tanker-flow analytics, energy-cargo intelligence, piracy-reporting bureaus. Operational data with measurable track records.
  • Tier 3 — Analytical institutions. Geopolitical and security think tanks with rigorous publication standards and demonstrable calibration history on this region.
  • Tier 4 — Commercial analysts & columnists. Independent practitioners and commodity-desk analysts whose published positions can be back-tested over multi-year horizons.
  • Tier 5 — Wire services & reputable regional press. Western wire services, top-tier business and political dailies, regional papers across the Gulf, Israel, and Iran (read with editorial-line caveats), and analytical defense-and-policy publications.
  • Tier 6 — Excluded by default. Anonymous OSINT accounts, op-eds, social media, "sources say" without attribution, AI-generated summaries, forum content. Enter the brief only after independent corroboration by Tier 1–3.
